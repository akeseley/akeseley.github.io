Reflection of my Journey.

From my past, to future goals.

I am very proud of the journey I have been on at this point in my life. I have had so many great experiences that have led me to where I am now. Going all the way back to high school, the choices I made back then have even had such a positive impact on me. When I joined Vantage, I didn’t know what I was getting myself into, but I soon found that I had gained presentation and project management skills unknowingly. This program also proved to me that although I didn’t want to be on the clinical side of healthcare, I still had a passion for health- related topics.

When I first came to college I majored in biology because I didn’t have any other idea of what I could do. After I realized there was a program in the College of Health Professions that emphasized leadership, I knew that that was going to be a better fit for me. Later that summer I worked at the Club at Old Hawthorne and continued to gain confidence in myself after moving to a new state. This serving job was more than a job to me since it forced me to gain both technical and interpersonal skills. During my freshman year, I didn’t know if I wanted to stay in Columbia for four years, but this job showed me that I could do anything.

During my sophomore year of college, I decided to add a business minor to my degree. I realized that working in a non-clinical setting in healthcare may mean working in a corporate setting, so a background in business concepts would be good to have. I soon realized that I loved my business classes and wanted to purse something along those lines. This summer I went back home to work and got a job at Edina Country Club. Although I wasn’t serving in a formal environment like in my last position, I learned how to bartend, which was a fun new skill to have.

When I started my junior year of college, I realized that I had extra room in my graduation plan and needed to either take random classes or I could add another element to my degree. This is when I found the IT certificate and realized it would align well with positions I want to obtain in the future. Because had a new interest in IT, I landed a role as a scrum master intern at Medica. This role was incredibly insightful for me as I got to learn the ins and outs of a business in the corporate world for the first time. This internship also taught me that I would succeed in data analyst or project management roles. With that being said, I am currently job searching and looking for entry level analyst roles in healthcare companies. My goal is to start in this type of technical position and eventually move up to a leadership position. I am very excited to land my first big job and start my career journey very soon.
